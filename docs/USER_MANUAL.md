## Introduction

### What is the Smart Civic System?

The Smart Civic Issue Reporting and Analytics System is a digital platform that enables citizens to report civic issues like potholes, garbage accumulation, fallen trees, and electrical problems directly to the municipal authorities. The system uses artificial intelligence to automatically categorize complaints and provides comprehensive analytics to help administrators efficiently manage and resolve issues.

### Key Features

- Easy complaint registration with image upload
- AI-powered automatic categorization of issues
- Real-time status tracking
- Interactive maps showing complaint locations
- Comprehensive analytics dashboard
- Multi-user role support
- Secure authentication system

### System Requirements

To use the Smart Civic System, you need:

- A modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge)
- Internet connection
- Valid email address for registration
- Camera or image files for photo evidence (optional)

## Getting Started

### Accessing the System

1. Open your web browser
2. Navigate to the system URL provided by your administrator
3. You will see the homepage with login and registration options

### Creating an Account (Citizens)

1. Click the "Register" or "Sign Up" button on the homepage
2. Fill in the registration form:
   - Full Name: Enter your complete name
   - Email Address: Provide a valid email
   - Password: Create a secure password (minimum 6 characters)
   - Phone Number: Enter your contact number (optional)
3. Click "Register" to create your account
4. You will be automatically logged in after successful registration

### Logging In

1. Click the "Login" button on the homepage
2. Enter your registered email address
3. Enter your password
4. Click "Login" to access your account

If you are an administrator or chief officer, use the credentials provided by your organization.

### Forgot Password

Password reset functionality may be available depending on your system configuration. Contact your system administrator if you cannot access your account.

## User Roles

### Citizen

Regular users who can report civic issues and track their complaints. Citizens can:
- Submit new complaints with descriptions and photos
- View their submitted complaints
- Track the status of their complaints
- Upvote other complaints to increase priority
- View basic analytics of their own activity

### Admin (Department Administrator)

Department-specific administrators responsible for managing complaints in their assigned area. Each admin manages one department:
- Garbage Admin
- Potholes Admin
- Electric Poles Admin
- Fallen Trees Admin

Admins can:
- View all complaints in their department
- Update complaint status
- Assign complaints to teams
- View department-specific analytics
- Generate reports

### Chief Officer

The highest-level administrator with system-wide access. The Chief Officer can:
- View all complaints across all departments
- Access complete system analytics
- Monitor overall system performance
- View cross-departmental statistics
- Generate comprehensive reports

## Citizen Features

### Dashboard Overview

### Creating a New Complaint

#### Method 1: Quick Submit Form

1. Navigate to "Report Issue" or click "New Complaint"
2. Fill in the form:
   - **Description**: Clearly describe the issue you are reporting
   - **Location**: Specify where the issue is located
   - **Category**: Select the type of issue:
     - Garbage: Waste accumulation, overflowing bins
     - Potholes: Road damage, uneven surfaces
     - Electric Poles: Damaged poles, exposed wires
     - Fallen Trees: Tree-related hazards
     - Miscellaneous: Other civic issues
   - **Image**: Upload a clear photo of the issue (optional but recommended)
3. Click "Submit Complaint"
4. You will receive a confirmation with a complaint ID

#### Method 2: Detailed Form

For more detailed reports:
1. Use the detailed complaint form
2. Additional fields include:
   - **Title**: Brief summary of the issue
   - **Ward Number**: Your area's ward number
   - **Severity Level**: 
     - Low: Minor inconvenience
     - Medium: Needs attention
     - High: Urgent issue
     - Critical: Immediate danger
   - **GPS Location**: Automatic or manual location marking

#### Tips for Effective Reporting

- Be specific and detailed in your description
- Include the exact location or nearby landmarks
- Upload clear, well-lit photos showing the issue
- Select the correct category for faster processing
- Provide your contact information for follow-ups
- Report genuine issues only

### Viewing Your Complaints

1. Navigate to "My Complaints" from the menu
2. You will see a list of all your submitted complaints
3. Each complaint shows:
   - Complaint ID and title
   - Category and status
   - Date submitted
   - Current status
   - Upvote count

### Complaint Status Meanings

- **Open**: Complaint has been registered and is pending review
- **In Progress**: The issue is being actively worked on
- **Resolved**: The issue has been fixed
- **Rejected**: The complaint was not accepted (with reason provided)

### Tracking Complaint Details

Click on any complaint to view full details:
- Complete description
- Photos uploaded
- Location on map
- Status history
- Assigned team or person
- Estimated resolution time
- Admin remarks

### Upvoting Complaints

If you see an issue affecting your area:
1. View the complaint details
2. Click the "Upvote" button
3. Higher upvoted complaints get increased priority

Note: You can only upvote each complaint once.

### Viewing Your Activity

Access "My Activity" to see:
- Total complaints submitted
- Resolution rate
- Average resolution time
- Most reported categories
- Your contribution to the community

## Admin Features

### Viewing Complaints

#### All Complaints View

1. Navigate to "Complaints" or "All Issues"
2. View all complaints assigned to your department
3. Use filters to narrow down:

#### Complaint Details

Click on any complaint to see:
- Citizen information (name, contact)
- Complete complaint description
- Photos and evidence
- Location on interactive map
- Status history timeline
- Previous actions taken
- Comments and notes

### Managing Complaints

#### Updating Status

1. Open a complaint
2. Click "Update Status"
3. Select new status:
   - In Progress: When work begins
   - Resolved: When issue is fixed
   - Rejected: If complaint is invalid (provide reason)
4. Add remarks explaining the update
5. Save changes

The citizen will be notified of status changes.

#### Assigning Complaints

1. Open a complaint
2. Click "Assign"
3. Enter the name of the team or person
4. Add any special instructions
5. Save assignment

This helps track who is responsible for resolution.

#### Adding Notes

Add internal notes to complaints for communication with other admins:
1. Open complaint details
2. Find "Admin Notes" section
3. Add your note
4. Save

Notes are visible to all admins but not to citizens.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: Is there a mobile app available?**
A: Currently, the system is web-based and works on mobile browsers. A dedicated app may be developed in the future.

**Q: How long does it take to resolve a complaint?**
A: Resolution time varies by issue type and severity. Average resolution times are displayed in the analytics section.

**Q: Can I submit anonymous complaints?**
A: No, you need to be registered and logged in to submit complaints. This ensures accountability.

**Q: What if I report the wrong category?**
A: The AI system will automatically detect and correct the category if needed. Admins can also recategorize if necessary.

**Q: Is my personal information safe?**
A: Yes, the system uses industry-standard security measures. Your information is only visible to authorized administrators.

### Citizen Questions

**Q: How do I know my complaint was received?**
A: You will see a confirmation message and receive a complaint ID immediately after submission.

**Q: Can I edit my complaint after submission?**
A: Currently, complaints cannot be edited. Contact the relevant department admin if corrections are needed.

**Q: Why was my complaint rejected?**
A: Check the admin remarks for the reason. Common reasons include duplicate reports, incorrect information, or issues outside municipal jurisdiction.

**Q: Can I delete my account?**
A: Contact the system administrator to request account deletion.

### Admin Questions

**Q: How do I assign multiple complaints at once?**
A: Use the bulk action feature in the complaints list view.

**Q: Can I export complaint data?**
A: Yes, use the export feature in the reports section to download data in CSV format.

**Q: What if a complaint is in the wrong department?**
A: Contact the Chief Officer to reassign the complaint to the correct department.

**Q: How often should I update complaint status?**
A: Update status whenever there is significant progress or at least once every few days for active complaints.

## Troubleshooting

### Login Issues

**Problem**: Cannot log in
- Check if email and password are correct
- Ensure caps lock is off
- Clear browser cache and cookies
- Try password reset if available

**Problem**: Forgot password
- Use the "Forgot Password" link
- Check your email for reset instructions
- Contact administrator if not working

### Complaint Submission Issues

**Problem**: Cannot upload image
- Check file size (must be under 10MB)
- Ensure image format is supported (JPEG, PNG, GIF)
- Try a different image
- Check internet connection

**Problem**: Form submission fails
- Check all required fields are filled
- Verify internet connection
- Try refreshing the page
- Clear browser cache

### Display Issues

**Problem**: Page not loading correctly
- Clear browser cache
- Disable browser extensions
- Try a different browser
- Check internet connection

**Problem**: Map not showing
- Enable location services
- Check browser permissions
- Refresh the page

### Performance Issues

**Problem**: System is slow
- Check your internet connection
- Close unnecessary browser tabs
- Clear browser cache
- Try during off-peak hours
